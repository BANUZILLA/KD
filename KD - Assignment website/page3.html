Early Career

Growing up, Durant played in the Amateur Athletic Union (AAU) for basketball where he played for several teams in Maryland, USA. Progressing into high school, he played two years of high school basketball at the National Christian Academy, one year at Oak Hill Academy and finished his senior year at the Montrose Christian School. In his later years at the University of Texas, Kevin was named the Washington Post All-Met Basketball Player of the Year and MVP of the 2006 McDonald's All-American Game.



College Career

In Durant's college season, he played for the Texas Longhorns, a team whose score that year was greatly boosted by Kevin alone whose natural skill and unmatched ability placed him as the unanimous national player of the year and won him several other prestigious awards. In fact, Durant was such an outstanding player that he only spent one year in college (2006-2007) before declaring for the NBA draft.



Professional Career

Officially starting his professional career as the second overall pick in the 2007 NBA draft by the Seattle SuperSonics, Durant debuted his first NBA game against the Denver Nuggets on October 31, 2007. From there, Durant's career only flourished and prospered as he moved on from the SuperSonics (which had changed to Oklahoma City Thunder) to the Golden State Warriors and then finally to the Brooklyn Nets, of whom he is still with to this day.



What Teams Has He Played For?



Formerly the Seattle SuperSonics, Durant played for Oklahoma City Thunder/ Seattle SuperSonics from 2007 to 2016.
Kevin then transferred to the Golden State Warriors whom he played for from 2016 to 2019.
Finally, he signed a deal with the Brooklyn Nets in 2019 and is currently still with them.
